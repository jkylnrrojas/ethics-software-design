Pan's Labyrinth Summary

Pan's Labyrinth is a 2006 dark fantasy film in Spanish, written, directed, and co-produced by Guillermo del Toro.

It follows a fairy tale in which an underworld princess visits the human realm. However, the sunlight from the human world blinds her and erases her memory, making her mortal. She dies, but her father believes her spirit will reincarnate and builds a labyrinth that acts as a portal between the realms, awaiting her return. Ofelia, the ten year old protagonist of the film, completes three tasks throughout the movie, all while dealing with the politics that come with the film's setting in 1944 Francoist Spain. After completing all the tasks, with the third being the hardest and costing Ofelia her life, the movie portrays her return to the underworld. The film states at the end that she went on to rule her kingdom with kindness and justice, and left traces of her time in the human realm "visible only to those who know where to look."
